# Stay Safe Online: A Prevention Guide for Students
**Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention Checklist**

---

## Digital Safety Checklist
As a Student Digital Ambassador, I recommend following these 8 essential steps to protect your digital life:

1.  **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Always use 2FA on your email, social media, and banking accounts to add a second layer of security beyond just a password.
2.  **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using "123456" or your birthdate. Use a mix of uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols for every different platform you use.
3.  **Verify Links Before Clicking:** Never click on suspicious links in SMS or emails (Phishing). Hover over a link to see the actual URL before clicking.
4.  **Keep Software Updated:** Regularly update your operating system and apps to ensure you have the latest security patches against malware.
5.  **Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or entering sensitive data while connected to free public Wi-Fi in cafes or stations.
6.  **Review Privacy Settings:** Periodically check the privacy settings on your Instagram, LinkedIn, and Facebook profiles to control who can see your personal information.

## Essential UPI & Financial Safety
7.  **PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving:** Remember that a UPI PIN is only required to **send** money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is a scam.
8.  **Check the Beneficiary Name:** Always double-check the name of the person or merchant on the confirmation screen before hitting the 'Pay' button to ensure the money goes to the right person.

## How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you encounter a scam or lose money to a fraudster, act immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** Visit [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) to file a formal complaint.
* **Helpline Number:** Call **1930** (24x7) to report financial fraud and initiate the process of freezing the stolen funds.
