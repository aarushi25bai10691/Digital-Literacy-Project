# Case Study: UPI "Collect Request" Social Engineering
**Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention**

---

## 1. What is UPI Fraud?
**Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud** is one of the most prevalent financial cybercrimes in India today. Unlike technical hacks that target software vulnerabilities, this crime primarily utilizes **social engineering** to deceive users. In this specific scenario, attackers exploit a victim's lack of technical awareness regarding the "Request Money" or "Collect" feature found in popular payment apps like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Instead of bypassing complex bank security, the criminal manipulates the user into voluntarily authorizing a transaction that drains their own bank account under the false guise of receiving a payment.

## 2. How the Crime Happens (Step-by-Step)
According to digital literacy standards, this fraud typically follows a structured psychological pattern designed to confuse and pressure the victim:

1.  **The Initial Hook:** The fraudster poses as a legitimate buyer on a marketplace like OLX or as a professional customer service agent from a well-known brand or bank.
2.  **The Deception:** The attacker claims they need to send a "pre-payment," "prize," or a "refund" to the victim. Instead of actually sending money, they initiate a **"Collect Request"** through their own UPI app.
3.  **The Psychological Trap:** The attacker calls the victim immediately, using high-pressure tactics to keep them engaged. They insist that the victim must click "Approve" and enter their **UPI PIN** to "verify" their identity or to "receive" the incoming funds.
4.  **The Unauthorized Debit:** The moment the victim enters their secret PIN, they are actually authorizing a debit. The funds are instantly moved from the victim's account to the attacker’s "mule" account.

## 3. Targeted Audience and Consequences
* **Primary Targets:** First-year college students, senior citizens, and small-scale online sellers are frequently targeted due to their potential unfamiliarity with how UPI mechanics work.
* **Financial and Personal Impact:** Victims face immediate financial loss, which can range from a few thousand rupees to their entire savings. Beyond the money, the consequences include significant emotional distress and a long-lasting loss of trust in digital banking platforms.
