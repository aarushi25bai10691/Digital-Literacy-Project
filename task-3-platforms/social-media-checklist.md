# 📱 Social Media Checklist for Digital Literacy

As a **Student Digital Ambassador**, maintaining a professional and secure social media presence is essential. Use this checklist to audit your digital footprint and ensure data integrity.

---

## ✅ The "Do's" (Professional Growth)

- [ ] **Audit Privacy Settings:** Review your account visibility (Public vs. Private) on platforms like Instagram and Facebook every 3 months.
- [ ] **Enable MFA/2FA:** Use an authenticator app (like Authy or Google Authenticator) for all professional accounts to prevent unauthorized access.
- [ ] **Showcase Technical Work:** Regularly share updates on AI/ML projects, Kaggle notebooks, or technical certifications to build your "Proof of Work."
- [ ] **Verify Before Sharing:** Before re-posting news or tech articles, cross-reference the information with official documentation to stop the spread of misinformation.
- [ ] **Maintain a Professional Bio:** Ensure your LinkedIn and GitHub bios are aligned, featuring a high-quality headshot and clear mention of your AI/ML focus at VIT Bhopal.

## ❌ The "Don'ts" (Security & Reputation)

- [ ] **Don't Share PII:** Never post "Personally Identifiable Information" like your phone number, home address, or photos of university ID/Hall tickets.
- [ ] **Don't Hardcode Secrets:** Avoid posting screenshots of code that contain visible API keys, database credentials, or private tokens.
- [ ] **Don't Engage with "Bot-Bait":** Ignore suspicious "Comment to win" posts or links promising free GPU/Cloud credits—these are common phishing traps.
- [ ] **Don't Vent Professionally:** Refrain from posting negative comments or "rants" about recruiters, professors, or peers that could damage your professional standing.
- [ ] **Don't Ignore Old Footprints:** Don't leave old, inactive accounts (from school days) searchable. Either delete them or update them to reflect your current professional level.

---

> **Note:** Digital Literacy is not just about using tools; it is about the responsible and ethical management of your digital identity.
